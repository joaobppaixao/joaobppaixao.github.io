About me

Hello! I am João Paixão, a Lisbon-based Multimedia Technician and Cinema & TV student at ETIC.

I started my journey in video editing when studying at EPI during my high-school years. There I discovered Video Editing, something that gave me enjoyment once only given by programming. This led me to produce Entr'aço, my first video essay as part of my PAP. In the end, with my Multimedia degree on hand, I entered into university to better my skills and to solidify my foundations.

When not exercising my knowledge on video editing I find myself producing videos for my main hobby, The Luso Urbanist, a Substack/YouTube publication on the subject of Urbanism and Public Transportation in Portugal. I have also recently challenged myself to improve my programming skills, which also led to the development of this website. Yes, this was coded by me.

Post PAP I had an internship at Beltrão Coelho, in their marketing department. I have also produced videos for some clients as a freelancer, as well as having been the Community Manager for SquigCraft where I managed updates, releases and all sorts of communication with the wider community

Entr'aço

Portugal's North Line is the unquestioned backbone of the railways in this country. Having been built over 100 year ago it posseses some limitations, both in terms of speed and capacity.

In this short video essay I explore what is the fix, why not everyone is in favour, and why I think it should move ahead.
